## Part 1: Conversion & Behavioral Science

### Eye Tracking & Attention
- **57%** of viewing time is spent above the fold (Nielsen Norman Group)
- **50ms** — Users form lasting visual appeal judgments in 50 milliseconds (Lindgaard et al., Carleton University). First impressions correlate with extended viewing.
- **2.5–15 seconds** — Extended first impression; speed and interaction design continue shaping trust.
- **75% credibility** — Stanford Web Credibility: aesthetic appeal drives perceived trust before content is read.
- **F-pattern** scanning: left-to-right from vertical axis — ideal for product grids
- **Z-pattern** scanning: diagonal across blocks — ideal for hero + CTA layouts. Place primary CTA where scan path lands.
- Viewing time above fold dropped from 80% (2010) to 57% (2018) — but still dominates

### Conversion Benchmarks
- Average eCommerce conversion rate: **3.65%** (top 10% >4.7%; luxury ~0.94%)
- Average bounce rate: **38–47%** overall; **51%** on mobile
- Mobile conversion: **2.9%** vs desktop **4.8%**; mobile **70%+** of traffic
- Homepage must answer "What's in it for me?" in **under 5 seconds**
- One store improved conversion from **2.1% to 3.8%** by repositioning visual hierarchy alone
- Above-fold infographics: **+6%** conversion; sticky add-to-cart **+5%** CVR
- Personalization: up to **300%** uplift when applied well

### Visual Hierarchy & Cognitive Load
- **System 1 vs. System 2** — Fast automatic attention vs. slow logical processing.
- **3–5 hierarchy levels** — Limit emphasis; avoid cognitive overload; one focus per section.
- **Gestalt** — Proximity, similarity, continuity; brains group elements before conscious awareness.
- Poor hierarchy depletes cognitive resources, increases errors, reduces conversion.

## Part 2: Social Proof & Trust

### Reviews & Ratings
- **93%** of consumers say online reviews impact purchase decisions
- **270%** conversion increase when products display 5+ reviews
- **67%** more purchases when customer reviews are visible
- **80%** conversion increase from video testimonials vs text
- **12.5%** conversion rate on pages with testimonials vs 11.4% without
- **86%** of buying decisions influenced by visible star ratings
- Optimal rating range: **4.0–4.7 stars** (perfect 5.0 hurts trust)

### Trust Badges & Signals
- Trust signals near CTAs increase conversion by up to **32%**
- Security badges near form fields outperform header/footer placement by **2–3x**
- 30-day money-back guarantee boosted sales by **32.57%**
- Unfamiliar badges **decreased trust by 12%** — recognition matters
- **19%** of cart abandonment from credit card security concerns
- **81%** of shoppers feel concerned on unfamiliar sites
- Store rating widgets missing from **76%** of stores (biggest easy win)

### Testimonial & Newsletter Placement
- Testimonials most effective **near CTAs**
- Moving signup to prominent header position increased conversions by **27.8%**
- Bottom-of-page captures **highly engaged visitors** who scrolled
- Short forms (name + email only) convert highest

## Part 3: Design Intelligence (2024–2026)

### Accessibility (Mandatory)
- **WCAG 2.2** — W3C Recommendation (Dec 2024). Use for maximum applicability.
- **Contrast** — 4.5:1 normal text, 3:1 large text (AA). AAA: 7:1.
- **Focus Not Obscured** — WCAG 2.2 AA: keyboard-focused items at least partially visible.
- **Colorblind** — 350M+ affected; avoid red/green, blue/purple; use patterns/icons with color.
- **prefers-reduced-motion** — Respect; provide static fallback.
- **EU Accessibility Act 2025** — WCAG 2.1 AA required for commercial digital products in EU.

### Color & Tokens
- **Token architecture** — Primitive → semantic → component. Never hardcode HEX in components.
- **60-30-10** — Dominant (60–70%), secondary (30%), accent (10%).
- **Avoid** — Pure black (#000) backgrounds; purple-on-white clichés.
- **2025 palettes** — Organic minimalism, digital lavender, modern luxury (rose gold, burgundy), warm neutrals.

### Typography (2025)
- **Fluid type** — CSS `clamp()` for responsive scaling.
- **Variable fonts** — Preferred for performance and flexibility.
- **Base 16–18px** — Minimum body; display 48–72px for hero.
- **Never:** Inter, Roboto, Open Sans, Lato, Arial, Space Grotesk.
- **Prefer:** Characterful display + refined body; serif renaissance for luxury.
- **Dark mode** — Slightly heavier weight, softer whites for readability.

### Motion & Performance
- **Microinteractions** — 120–200ms; **10–15%** conversion uplift.
- **Transitions** — 240–400ms; hero up to 700ms.
- **Respect** — `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **Core Web Vitals** — LCP ≤2.5s, INP ≤200ms, CLS <0.1. Good CWV: ~18% conversion lift, 32% engagement.

### Layout & Rhythm
- **Visual rhythm** — Establish rhythm, then deliberately break it; alternate full-width and contained sections.
- **Never two full-bleed adjacent** — FULL → contained(1–3) → FULL.
- **Containers** — Max-width 1200–1440px; 24–48px padding.
- **Mobile-first** — 60%+ traffic; large tap targets, fast load.

## Part 4: Luxury & Premium Aesthetics

### Top Brand Patterns (Rolex, Patek Philippe, Omega, AP)
- **Heritage/story** is central — not optional
- Product showcased through **immersive photography**, not e-commerce grids only
- **Brand-first layouts** — aesthetic over convention
- **Museum, manufacture, artisan sections** build aspiration
- **Service commitment** (e.g. lifetime warranty) as selling point
- **Retail locator** drives offline conversion

### Luxury Homepage Must-Haves
1. Cinematic hero (video preferred)
2. Featured collections (not just "all products")
3. Best sellers / iconic models
4. Heritage / brand story block
5. Craftsmanship storytelling
6. Lifestyle banner
7. Trust / credentials (authenticity, materials, shipping)
8. Editorial / journal (SEO + authority)
9. Testimonials / reviews
10. Newsletter with "exclusive" positioning
11. Footer with store locator, service, legal

### Quiet Luxury Design
- Minimalism, restraint, precision over excess
- Asymmetric grids, editorial whitespace
- Cinematic, high-res imagery; every visual serves the narrative
- **Impact** — +60% engagement, +40% time on site; 70% say site influences brand perception

## Part 5: AIDA Applied to Homepages

| Stage      | Goal        | Sections                          | Key metric        |
|-----------|-------------|------------------------------------|-------------------|
| **Attention** | Stop the scroll | Hero, Credentials bar              | Bounce rate       |
| **Interest**  | Explore further | Categories, Products               | Scroll depth, CTR |
| **Desire**    | Want it       | Banner, Story, Trust               | Time on page, ATC |
| **Action**    | Convert       | Journal, Testimonials, Newsletter  | Conversion, signup |
